RJ

A robotic, natural interaction display

  • Software Development
  • UX Design

Developed throughout the summer of 2013, RJ is an interactive robotic display that lets people control a 6-foot-tall robot using their own body movements.

  • GitHub Repository

Background

When the Neuroscience and Robotics Lab (NxR) at Nortwhestern University purchased Baxter, a humanoid robot built by Rethink Robotics, they faced the challenge of developing a demo to captivate and engage the Northwestern community. After multiple brainstorming sessions and code sprints, we decided to use natural interaction technology to create that display. The Technology

One of my primary focuses when working on RJ was to mask the software architecture's complexity so that users wouldn't have to worry about what was actually going on in the background. The software stack uses Python, C++, the Point Cloud Library (PCL), and the Robot Operating System (ROS).

  • People make wild gestures.
  • The Asus Xtion Pro uses infrared and depth sesnsors to convert the gestures to a matrix of points in space.
  • ROS nodes runnning on a desktop machine convert those points into vectors representing the person's body parts.
  • RJ folows the positions on those vectors while also making sure to avoid collisions with any objects in his vicinity.

End Result

RJ was finally put on display in the Willens Lounge of the Technological Institute at Northwestern University in the Fall of 2013. Two years later, he is still a popular attraction for patrons of the engineering building as well as university visitors year round.
