The Book

A fraternity recruitment platform

  • Front-End Development
  • UI Design
  • UX Design

Replacing an outdated system, The Book is a tool used to consolidate potential new member (PNM) information and streamline the complex fraternity recruitment process.

  • GitHub Repository

Background

Every year, a fraternity chapter meets upwards of a couple hundred PNMs throughout the recruitment season. With a chapter membership of over a hundred brothers, keeping track of all the interactions and impressions that take place throughout that perios is valuable, yet very difficult. With the existing system repleet with glitches, usability issues, and limited capabilities, the Sigma Chi chapter at Northwestern needed an upgrade, and over the summer of 2013, Alex Kaldjian and I took it upon ourselves to develop a new platform.

Splash page for browsing PNMs

The Technology

While Alex took on the backend development using Ruby on Rails, I put together a lightweight front-end using the following tools.

  • jQuery
  • HTML5
  • Sass
  • CSS3
Masonry grid of PNMs

Browsing PNMs

One of The Book's goals is to give as much face time as possible to every PNM. To do this, I used Masonry, a Javascript grid layout library, to present the PNMs in a random order every time a brother visited the browse page.

Individual PNM Pages

The primary use case for The Book is to share snipets that reveal a PNM's character and personality. PNMs each have a page devoted to them that highlights these traits, consolidating that information for the brotherhood.

Individual PNM page
Portion of the news feed

News Feed

While it's great to have a place to hold information on all the interactions we have with PNMs, painstakingly browsing every PNM page to check for new information isn't efficient. Instead, on The Book's main browse page, we feature a newws feed that displays the most recent brother activity so that users can stay up to date on the most relevant information.

End Result

This is the second year that The Book has been used. With more than a hundred active members, the application has proved to be robust and effective, allowing the chapter to wield a greater control over the recruitment process.
